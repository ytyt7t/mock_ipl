HOW WE COUNT POINTS?

Points are assigned to teams based on the results of their matches. A win typically awards a team the highest number of points, often two points, indicating their success in the game. In betting, teams with more points are seen as stronger, making them favoured choices for bettors predicting future match outcomes. The process of counting points in cricket tournaments involves specific rules and intricacies that contribute to the overall competitiveness and fairness of the event. Understanding these peculiarities is crucial for fans and enthusiasts to assess team standings accurately and follow the progress of their favourite teams. The points system for cricket tournaments can vary depending on the specific rules of the tournament. In the majority of tournaments, teams are awarded 2 points for a victory, 1 point for a draw, and 0 points for a loss. Additionally, certain tournaments incorporate bonus points as a means to acknowledge exceptional achievements, such as scoring a specific number of runs or taking a certain number of wickets The points distribution may vary across different formats of the game. In Test cricket, for instance, points are often allocated based on the result of the match.
